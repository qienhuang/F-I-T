## PAGE 1 - CORE STRUCTURE

### 1) One-sentence definition

> FIT is a phase-structured framework for explaining how stable structures emerge, persist, and become effectively irreversible in complex systems, through the interaction of Force, Information, Time, and Constraint.

### 2) Minimal variables

**Force (F)**  
A propagating driver of change that can affect non-local system states. If Force cannot propagate, only local perturbations exist.

**Information (I)**  
Structures that persist across time and influence future dynamics. Transient patterns are not Information by default.

**Time (T)**  
The direction and rhythm of updates. Time selects which structures survive (a stability filter).

**Constraint (C)** (derived)  
Accumulated stable information that shrinks the reachable state space.

### 3) Phase as a first-class object

> A Phase (Phi) is not a time segment, but a dynamical regime defined by how F, I, and C interact (EST-scoped).

Canonical phases (minimal basis):

- **Phi1 - Accumulation**: Force exists; structure does not persist. Exploration dominates.
- **Phi2 - Crystallization**: local structures stabilize; coordination is incomplete.
- **Phi3 - Coordination**: Force is absorbed; Information is reusable; Constraints dominate; a stable operating regime emerges.

Phases may be nested, parallel, or restarted across scales.

### 4) Phase transition: Minimal Signal Set (PT-MSS)

A phase transition is registered only if all three signal classes appear together within a declared observation window:

1. **Force redistribution** - how Force propagates changes  
2. **Information re-encoding** - information carriers/representations shift  
3. **Constraint reorganization** - constraint proxies reorganize non-smoothly  

Single signals are never sufficient.

### 5) Irreversibility (precise meaning)

> Irreversibility does not mean "no change", but that large-scale structural rollback becomes increasingly unlikely in Phi3 under declared estimators.

Stability is not optimality. Stability is not permanence.

### 6) Boundary conditions (when FIT applies)

FIT applies only if all are true:

1. Force is propagatable (non-local)
2. Information persists across time
3. The state space is finite and compressible (Constraint is meaningful)

Otherwise, FIT is not applicable.

### 7) Red lines (what FIT is not)

- Not a forecasting tool
- Not a value or moral theory
- Not a guarantee of progress
- Not historical inevitability

FIT reduces possibility space; it does not choose outcomes.

## PAGE 2 - POST-PHI3 FUTURES (THE BIFURCATION)

### 8) The core insight

> Entering Phi3 is not "success". It is arrival at a structural bifurcation point.

After Phi3, only two structurally viable paths exist.

### 9) Why Phi3 necessarily bifurcates

- Stability strengthens constraints
- Strong constraints suppress exploration
- Suppressed exploration reduces adaptability

Thus Phi3 creates an internal tension between stability and future viability.

### 10) Path A - Coordination collapse

**Phi3 -> structural decline**

Triggered when any two occur:

- **Constraint hardening**: rules grow faster than learning/renewal
- **Force homogenization**: more of the same Force is applied to new challenges
- **Information ossification**: knowledge stabilizes but cannot regenerate

Outcome:

Short-term stability -> diminishing returns -> structural breakdown or displacement.

This is the most common historical outcome.

### 11) Path B - Hierarchical phase transition

**Phi3 -> Phi4(Phi1)**  
A higher-level exploration begins while lower-level stability remains.

All three must hold simultaneously:

- **(B1) Force uplift**: a new Force emerges at a higher abstraction level, not directly competing at the same level
- **(B2) Information re-stratification**: Information shifts from execution rules to rule-generating structures
- **(B3) Constraint enveloping**: old constraints are preserved and embedded as sub-constraints

Outcome:

Surface stability persists; a new layer begins exploration; cross-generational adaptability emerges.

Note: `Phi4(Phi1)` is notation for "a higher-level Phi1 starts", not a new primitive and not a core version bump.

### 12) The one-line diagnostic

> Is the system deliberately creating an exploration space that threatens its own current stability?

- No -> Path A is overwhelmingly likely
- Yes, and it survives -> Path B becomes possible

### 13) Common misreadings (avoid)

- Scaling is not transition
- Efficiency is not evolution
- Rule tightening is not governance success

These are often Phi3 internal optimizations, not exits.

### 14) Final structural statement

> After Phi3 there is no destiny. Only structural choice.
